ABOUT ME

Joshua Sobel has been an avid organist for more than a decade. Born in November 2001, he showed a very early interest in the pipe organ, and began playing piano and singing in the Church choir at age 3. By the age of 5, he was able to play back nearly any melody by ear, and was already capable of complex improvisation. He began his piano studies at the Cary School of Music in 2008.

His organ studies began in 2010, with the arrival of a small chamber organ at his childhood home. He took private lessons, first with Josh Dumbleton, the organist at his church, and then with Dr. Mark Andersen, a former student of Marcel Dupre and E. Power Biggs. He has won numerous prizes in repertoire and hymn performance.

Joshua is currently persuing a degree in Organ Performance under Dr. Timothy Olsen at the University of North Carolina School of the Arts, and serves as Organ Scholar at Centenary UMC in Winston-Salem, NC. He also serves as Co-Artistic Director of the Maurice Duruflé Academy of Music, as well as being the chief recording engineer for Synesthete Recordings, its recording division.

Joshua Sobel


Joshua has been praised by adjudicators for his "ease and confidence at the keyboard," (The American Organist, 2018); and his skill as a recording engineer have led to him being asked to produce recordings for, among others, the American Guild of Organists, the Organ Historical Society, and Oxford University Music Press.
